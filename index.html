About the company

A documentation-driven importer, not a generic freight promise.

Eve Las Enterprise is a Philippine trading and importing business based in Putatan, Muntinlupa City. Public government records identify the company as an NCR commercial importer listed by the Bureau of Fisheries and Aquatic Resources and as a registered importer with the Bureau of Plant Industry–National Plant Quarantine Services Division for specified plants and planting materials.

Our operating principle is simple: compliance before shipment. Commodity scope, species or variety, source country, intended use, destination facility, permits, broker instructions, inspection conditions, costs, and risk allocation must be clear before cargo moves.

Controlled workflow

Every shipment starts with verification—not assumptions.

  1. 01

    Screen the requirement

    Confirm the commodity, scientific name or variety, condition, quantity, value, origin, supplier, purpose, port, and final destination.

  2. 02

    Map permits and responsibilities

    Identify customs, fisheries, plant, animal, wildlife, food, or local approvals and assign who obtains each document.

  3. 03

    Verify the supplier file

    Review invoices, packing lists, origin records, health or phytosanitary certificates, and export permissions before departure.

  4. 04

    Coordinate freight and entry

    Align handling instructions, route, broker file, tariff description, valuation support, permits, and arrival notices.

  5. 05

    Support inspection and release

    Coordinate presentation to government authorities, respond to findings, monitor charges, and control onward delivery.

  6. 06

    Close and retain the record

    Reconcile permits, duties, taxes, delivery records, condition reports, incidents, and client confirmation.

Regulatory and privacy notice

Import eligibility depends on the facts of each shipment.

Requirements depend on current laws, agency rules, tariff classification, commodity description, species or variety, condition, source country, supplier, intended use, port, destination, facility suitability, and shipment-specific documents.

Eve Las Enterprise is not a government agency and does not control permit approval, customs valuation, taxes, examination, seizure, quarantine action, release timing, carrier delay, storage, demurrage, or the survival and condition of living commodities.

Private company records

DTI, BIR, Mayor’s Permit, private BOC records, original certificates, financial documents, proprietor identification, signatures, contracts, and facility files must be attached and verified by the company before formal government submission.
